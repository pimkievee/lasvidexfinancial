Health Care Directives Explained

Health care and personal care planning can reduce uncertainty for loved ones during medical or end-of-life decisions.

Last updated: May 25, 2026

General information only: This resource is educational and is not legal or medical advice. Personal care and health directive documents should be reviewed with qualified professionals in your province or territory.

What health directives are

Health care directives, personal care directives, or related planning documents can communicate who should make care decisions and what wishes should guide those decisions if you cannot speak for yourself.

Why they matter

Without clear direction, family members may face difficult decisions with limited guidance. A written plan can reduce stress, conflict, and uncertainty at an already emotional time.

End-of-life planning

Health directive conversations may include preferences around care, comfort, life-sustaining treatment, spiritual values, family involvement, and the kind of decision-making process you want respected.

Protecting family from difficult decisions

The goal is not only documentation. It is also to give loved ones clarity, so they do not have to guess what you would have wanted.

Health directive planning often connects with POA planning, estate planning basics, and beneficiary reviews.
